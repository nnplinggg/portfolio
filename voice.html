I came to storytelling through two very different lenses — a camera and a courtroom argument.

Before I learned to manage products, I learned to see. As a documentary photographer in the mountains of northern Vietnam, I trained myself to sit with a moment before reaching for the shutter — to feel what a place held before trying to capture it. That instinct, to slow down and listen before you speak, is the same one I bring to research, to user interviews, to every conversation where someone is trying to be understood.

The courtroom came later. As a competitive debater for six years, then a national coach, I learned that a story only lands when you know your audience. Persuasion isn't decoration — it is the delivery system for truth. If you can't make people feel why something matters, the idea dies in the room.

These two threads — seeing and arguing — are what make me a storyteller. I don't write for writing's sake. I write because clarity is a form of care, and because the people I work for deserve to be heard.

"Clarity is a form of care."

03  /  Documentary Photography

The Souls of Mountains

Ha Giang, Vietnam · 2022

A solo documentary project in the remote highlands of Ha Giang. Three weeks, no translator, 400+ frames. I lived alongside Hmong families during their spring harvest — not to capture the exotic, but to find the ordinary: laughter over shared meals, tired hands, children who ran from the camera then came back.

The project was selected for exhibition at the Vietnam Youth Photography Festival, 2022. A short accompanying essay was published in Nhân Dân Weekend.

2022

Solo exhibition, Vietnam Youth Photography Festival — "Souls of Mountains" series (12 prints)

2022

Published photo essay, Nhân Dân Weekend — "What the Mountains Hold" (6,000 words + 18 images)

2021

Top 10 finalist, National Geographic Student Photo Award (Vietnam)
